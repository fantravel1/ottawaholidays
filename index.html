What Makes Ottawa Different

Five Cities in One

Ottawa isn't one thing. It's a layered, surprising, and deeply livable city that reveals itself season by season, neighborhood by neighborhood.

River City

Two rivers, a UNESCO canal, and over 50 km of waterfront pathways. Ottawa's relationship with water defines its character, from kayaking the Ottawa River to walking the Rideau Canal.

Bike City

Over 800 kilometres of dedicated cycling paths weave through the city, along rivers, through forests, and past national landmarks. Ottawa is one of North America's most bike-friendly capitals.

Museum City

Canada's museum capital. With over 30 museums and galleries — including seven national museums — Ottawa offers more cultural depth per square kilometre than almost anywhere in Canada.

Winter-Play City

While other cities hibernate, Ottawa celebrates. The Rideau Canal becomes the world's largest skating rink, Winterlude brings ice sculptures and snow playgrounds, and cozy cafes glow with warmth.

Slow Elegance

Ottawa doesn't rush. It's a city built for long walks, lingering coffees, and unexpected discoveries. Elegant without pretension, lively without chaos. Canada, in slow motion.

Signature Experiences

Things You Can Only Do in Ottawa

From skating to work on a frozen canal to watching the sun set behind Parliament Hill from a kayak — these are the experiences that make Ottawa unforgettable.

Winter

Skate the Rideau Canal

The world's largest naturally frozen skating rink — a UNESCO World Heritage Site that transforms into 7.8 km of pure winter joy every January through March. Grab a BeaverTail along the way.
Spring

Tulip Festival

Over one million tulips bloom across the capital every May, the legacy of a gift from the Netherlands after WWII. Commissioner's Park becomes a sea of colour.
Year-Round

ByWard Market

One of Canada's oldest and largest public markets. Browse artisan shops, taste local food, and feel the energy of Ottawa's most vibrant neighbourhood, from dawn to well past dark.
Culture

National Museums

Seven national museums, including the Canadian Museum of History, the National Gallery, and the War Museum. More cultural depth per square km than almost anywhere in Canada.
Active

Bike the River Pathways

Follow the Ottawa River Pathway, pedal along the Rideau Canal, or take on scenic loops through Gatineau Park. 800+ km of paths await, through forests, past landmarks, and along the water.
Nature

River Sunsets

Watch the sun dip behind Parliament Hill from the Ottawa River. Paddle, walk, or simply sit and witness one of Canada's most spectacular urban sunsets.

Explore by Neighbourhood

Every Corner Has a Story

Ottawa's neighbourhoods each have their own personality. From the energy of ByWard Market to the creative pulse of Hintonburg, discover the local side of the capital.

ByWard Market

Food • Nightlife • Artisan Shops

Ottawa's beating heart. Canada's oldest farmers' market surrounded by restaurants, boutiques, pubs, and galleries. Electric day and night.

The Glebe

Walkable • Local • Boutiques

Tree-lined Bank Street, independent shops, cozy brunch spots, and Lansdowne Park. The Glebe is Ottawa's perfectly walkable neighbourhood.

Hintonburg

Creative • Cafes • Art

Ottawa's creative district. Specialty coffee roasters, indie galleries, vintage shops, and some of the city's best restaurants on Wellington West.

Centretown

Central • Elegant • Connected

Ottawa's downtown core. Steps from Parliament Hill, the canal, and national museums. Elegant dining, city parks, and the best of urban Ottawa.

Old Ottawa South

Parks • Community • Relaxed

A leafy, relaxed neighbourhood along the Rideau Canal. Great for walks, family outings, and discovering local coffee shops and bookstores.

Common Questions

Planning Your Ottawa Trip

What is the best time to visit Ottawa?

Ottawa is a true four-season destination, and the "best" time depends on what you love. Winter (December–March) offers Rideau Canal skating and Winterlude. Spring (April–May) brings the famous Tulip Festival with over a million blooms. Summer (June–August) is festival season with Canada Day celebrations on Parliament Hill. Fall (September–November) showcases stunning foliage in Gatineau Park. Each season has its own magic, and Ottawa rewards repeat visits.

Is Ottawa worth visiting for a weekend?

Absolutely. Ottawa is perfectly sized for a weekend getaway. In 2–3 days you can explore world-class museums, skate the Rideau Canal (in winter), walk the ByWard Market, bike along the river, and discover charming neighbourhoods like The Glebe and Hintonburg. It's genuinely the most balanced city weekend in Canada — enough to fill your days without ever feeling rushed.

What are the best neighbourhoods to explore?

Ottawa's best neighbourhoods include ByWard Market (food, nightlife, and artisan shops), The Glebe (walkable, local boutiques, and cafes), Hintonburg (creative scene and specialty coffee), Centretown (central and elegant), and Old Ottawa South (parks and community energy). Each neighbourhood has its own distinct vibe, and they're all easy to reach on foot or by bike.

What museums should I visit in Ottawa?

Ottawa is Canada's museum capital. Must-visits include the Canadian Museum of History (Canada's most-visited museum), the National Gallery of Canada (featuring the iconic Maman spider sculpture), the Canadian War Museum, the Canadian Museum of Nature, and the Canada Aviation and Space Museum. Most are within easy reach of downtown, and many offer free Thursday evening admission.

Can you bike around Ottawa easily?

Yes! Ottawa has over 800 km of multi-use pathways, making it one of the most bike-friendly cities in North America. Popular routes include the Ottawa River Pathway, the Rideau Canal Eastern Pathway, and scenic routes through Gatineau Park. Bike rentals are widely available downtown, and many hotels offer bike-sharing partnerships. The city is flat enough for casual riders and scenic enough for dedicated cyclists.

How do I get around Ottawa without a car?

Ottawa is very manageable without a car. The O-Train Confederation Line connects key areas of the city. OC Transpo buses cover the broader metro area. For day-to-day exploring, walking and cycling are the best ways to experience neighbourhoods. Ride-sharing services are available, and many of Ottawa's top attractions are clustered in the walkable downtown core. For Gatineau Park day trips, the STO bus system connects across the river.

Beyond the City

Day Trips from Ottawa

Ottawa is the perfect base for exploring the surrounding region. From Gatineau Park's wilderness to the charm of small Ontario and Quebec towns.

Gatineau Park

15 min drive

361 square kilometres of protected wilderness. Hiking, swimming, skiing, and the most spectacular fall colours in the region.

Wakefield

30 min drive

A picturesque village in the Gatineau Hills. Famous for its covered bridge, artisan community, and the iconic Wakefield Mill.

Almonte

45 min drive

A heritage textile town turned artisan hub. Beautiful Mill Street, independent shops, and one of Ontario's most charming main streets.

Perth

1 hr drive

One of Ontario's prettiest towns. Heritage architecture, the Tay Canal, craft breweries, and a remarkably vibrant food scene.
